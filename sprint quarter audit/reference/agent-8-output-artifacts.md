# Agent 8 — Output Artifacts (8A–8D)

Four audience-calibrated outputs. Each is produced from the same underlying
audit data, reformatted for a different reader with different needs.

---

# 8A — Team Sprint Retro Brief

**Audience:** The engineering team + PM. Used in the retro meeting.
**Tone:** Direct, collaborative, action-oriented. Not sanitised.
**Length:** 1 page. Can be read in the meeting room in 3 minutes.

```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SPRINT RETRO BRIEF — [Sprint Name] | [Date]
Generated by PM Sprint Audit | Not a substitute for the conversation
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

SPRINT IN NUMBERS
─────────────────
Delivered:      [X%] of planned ([N]/[N pts])
Unplanned work: [X%] of capacity ([N pts])
Carryover:      [N] tickets ([N] were here last sprint too)
Composition:    [X%] feature | [X%] bugs | [X%] debt | [X%] reactive

WHAT WENT WELL
──────────────
✅ [Finding 1 — specific, evidence-grounded]
✅ [Finding 2]
✅ [Finding 3]

WHAT NEEDS A CONVERSATION
──────────────────────────
⚠️ [Issue 1 — specific, non-blaming, with evidence]
   Evidence: [data point]
   Question to discuss: [specific question]

⚠️ [Issue 2]
   Evidence: [data point]
   Question to discuss: [specific question]

⚠️ [Issue 3]
   Evidence: [data point]
   Question to discuss: [specific question]

OKR PULSE
─────────
[KR1]: [moved +X% / flat / moved away] — [one sentence on what drove this]
[KR2]: [moved +X% / flat / moved away]
Orphaned work (no OKR link): [X%] of sprint — discuss: is this OK?

THE CHRONIC CARRYOVER
──────────────────────
[TICKET-ID]: "[Title]" — now in its [N]th sprint
Discuss: What specifically needs to happen to close this?

NEXT SPRINT WATCH
─────────────────
Based on this sprint's data, we should watch for: [2–3 specific things]

[PM to add context: _______________]
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

# 8B — Executive Audit Report

**Audience:** CPO, VP Product, or CEO. Used in quarterly business reviews,
leadership updates, or when a stakeholder asks "how is the team doing?"
**Tone:** Strategic, outcome-focused, measured. No ticket-level detail.
**Length:** 2 pages maximum. Structured for 5-minute read.

```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PRODUCT TEAM AUDIT — [Sprint/Quarter] | [Date]
[SPRINT AUDIT / QUARTER AUDIT]
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (90-second read)
────────────────────────────────────
[2–3 paragraph narrative. Answers: what did the team commit to,
 what did they deliver, what moved in the product metrics, and what
 is the one most important thing leadership should know?

 Written in plain language. No PM jargon. No ticket IDs.
 Does not hide bad news — states it clearly with context.]

STRATEGIC DELIVERY
───────────────────
Roadmap commitments for period:     [N] features
Delivered as planned:               [N] ([X%])
Deferred or dropped:                [N] — [key items and brief reason]
Unplanned work consumed:            [X%] of team capacity

Strategic narrative integrity:
  [DELIVERED — quarter's work advanced stated objective]
  [DILUTED — objective partially advanced; reactive work consumed X% of capacity]
  [PIVOTED — team shifted focus intentionally/unintentionally; context below]

[If diluted or pivoted: one paragraph of context from PM]

OKR PROGRESS
─────────────
[Objective 1: statement]
  KR1: [metric] [X → Y] | Target: [Z] | Status: [On track / At risk / Behind]
  KR2: [metric] [X → Y] | Target: [Z] | Status: [On track / At risk / Behind]

[Objective 2: statement]
  [same format]

Overall OKR health: [On track / Mixed / At risk] — [one sentence explanation]

PRODUCT QUALITY
───────────────
Bug backlog: [improved / stable / worsened — by how much]
Quality trend: [3-sprint direction]
Post-launch issues: [none noted / [N] tickets on [feature] — being addressed]

TEAM VELOCITY
─────────────
6-sprint velocity trend: [improving / stable / declining]
Current sprint capacity utilisation: [X% productive vs X% reactive]
Most significant pattern: [one finding from Agent 6]

RISKS AND WATCH ITEMS
──────────────────────
🔴 [Critical risk — one sentence]
🟡 [Watch item — one sentence]
🟡 [Watch item — one sentence]

[Items where PM context was incorporated: noted below each finding]

RECOMMENDED LEADERSHIP ACTIONS
────────────────────────────────
[If any findings require a leadership decision or conversation to unblock.
 Only include if the audit surfaced something that requires stakeholder input
 — e.g., a strategic trade-off that can't be resolved at the PM level.]
  1. [Specific action / decision]
  2. [Specific action / decision]
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

# 8C — Next Sprint Calibration Inputs

**Audience:** PM + engineering team. Used in sprint planning.
**Tone:** Practical, forward-looking, evidence-based.
**Length:** Half a page. Planning reference, not a document to read.

```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
NEXT SPRINT CALIBRATION — Based on [Sprint Name] Audit
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

CAPACITY ADJUSTMENT
────────────────────
This sprint's actual productive capacity: [N pts]
Adjustment for next sprint:

  Estimated unplanned work buffer:  [X%] (based on [N]-sprint average of [X%])
  Carryover tickets to account for: [N pts] ([list ticket IDs])
  Known blockers heading in:        [list any known external dependencies]

  RECOMMENDED PLANNED CAPACITY: [N pts]
  (vs. nominal team capacity of [N pts] — adjustment: -[X pts] for buffer + carryover)

ESTIMATION CALIBRATIONS
────────────────────────
[If estimation bias detected:]
  [Ticket type] has been consistently [under/over]-estimated by ~[X%]
  Recommend: adjust estimates for [type] tickets by [direction]

[If no bias detected:]
  Estimation appears calibrated — maintain current approach

COMPOSITION TARGETS
────────────────────
Recommended next sprint composition based on current trends:
  New features:  [X%] (adjust from [current X%] because [reason])
  Enhancements:  [X%]
  Bug fixes:     [X%] (current backlog has [N] bugs — [priority level])
  Tech debt:     [X%]

[If bug backlog grew this sprint: recommend allocating additional bug fix capacity]

CARRY-IN ITEMS (Must address)

[TICKET-ID]: "[Title]" — [N sprints old] — handle by: [complete / explicitly defer / kill]

SPECIFIC WATCH ITEMS FOR NEXT SPRINT
──────────────────────────────────────
☐ [Action 1 — specific, with owner suggestion]
☐ [Action 2]
☐ [Action 3]

OKR FOCUS
──────────
KRs that need work in next sprint (were underfunded this sprint):
  [KR name]: received [X%] of capacity this sprint, needs [Y%] to hit target by [date]
  [KR name]: [same format]
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

# 8D — Quarter Retrospective Report (QUARTER AUDIT ONLY)

**Audience:** PM + leadership. Used at quarter close, board prep, or
annual planning. The document that answers: "What actually happened in Q[N]?"
**Tone:** Comprehensive, honest, strategically framed.
**Length:** 3–5 pages. Complete narrative with supporting data.

```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
QUARTER [N] RETROSPECTIVE — [Product/Team Name]
[Date range] | Generated by PM Sprint Audit
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

PART 1: THE QUARTER IN BRIEF
─────────────────────────────
[3–4 paragraph executive narrative. Written for a reader who wasn't
 in the weeds. Tells the story of the quarter: what was committed,
 what changed, what was delivered, and what the metrics show.
 Includes the honest assessment of where the quarter fell short and why.]

PART 2: STRATEGIC DELIVERY
────────────────────────────
Roadmap commitments: [N features]
  Delivered on time:         [N] ([X%])
  Delivered late:            [N] ([X%])
  Deferred to next quarter:  [N] ([X%]) — [list with brief reason each]
  Dropped/cancelled:         [N] ([X%]) — [list with brief reason each]
  Delivered but unplanned:   [N] ([X%]) — [list]

Roadmap fidelity: [X%]
Strategic narrative: [delivered / diluted / drifted / pivoted]

What we committed to (quarter start):
  "[Stated objective or roadmap theme]"

What we actually delivered (quarter end):
  "[Honest assessment of what the quarter produced]"

The gap (if any): [Description of drift, with root cause where known]

PART 3: OKR OUTCOMES
──────────────────────
[For each OKR:]

OBJECTIVE [N]: [Statement]
  Quarter start → Quarter end:
  
  KR1: [metric] [start X → end Y | target Z]
    Progress: [X% of target achieved]
    Primary work that drove this: [2–3 features / sprints that contributed]
    What didn't move this and why: [honest assessment]
    
  KR2: [same format]

Overall OKR quarter result:
  Achieved: [N/N KRs within striking distance of target]
  At risk:  [N KRs]
  Missed:   [N KRs — with honest root cause]

PART 4: OPERATIONAL HEALTH
────────────────────────────
Velocity over quarter: [chart data in text — Sprint N: X pts → Sprint N+1: Y pts → ...]
Velocity trend: [improving / stable / declining] — [cause if known]

Composition across quarter:
  Feature work: [X% avg across quarter]
  Bug fixes:    [X% avg — trend: [improving/worsening]]
  Reactive:     [X% avg — trend: [improving/worsening]]

Quality: [Bug backlog change over quarter: +N / -N / stable]

PART 5: PATTERNS AND LEARNINGS
────────────────────────────────
SYSTEMIC FINDINGS (appeared in 3+ of the quarter's sprints):
  1. [Finding] — Root cause: [if known] — Recommended fix: [action]
  2. [Finding] — [same format]

WHAT WORKED THIS QUARTER:
  [Evidence-grounded positive findings — not just "the team worked hard"]

WHAT DIDN'T WORK AND WHY:
  [Honest assessment — grounded in data from agents 2–7]

PART 6: NEXT QUARTER INPUTS
─────────────────────────────
Carry-forward items needing resolution:
  [TICKET/FEATURE] — [N sprints/weeks old] — decision needed: [complete/defer/kill]

Capacity calibration for next quarter:
  Based on actual Q[N] velocity of [X pts/sprint avg]:
  Q[N+1] planned capacity: [N pts/sprint × N sprints = N total pts]
  Buffer recommendation: [X%] for unplanned work (Q[N] ran at [X% unplanned avg])

Strategic focus recommendation:
  [Based on audit: where should Q[N+1] concentrate given what Q[N] showed?]

OKRs needing reset or re-commitment:
  [List any KRs that are clearly off-track and need leadership discussion]

PM CONTEXT AND NOTES
──────────────────────
[Section for PM to add org context, explanations, and any findings
 where the data alone doesn't tell the full story]

_________________________________________________________
Generated: [date]
Audit mode: Quarter | Sprints analysed: [N] | Data sources: [list]
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

By dimension:
  Classification accuracy: [X/5] | Description quality:  [X/5]
  AC coverage:             [X/5] | Grooming depth:        [X/5]
  Estimation calibration:  [X/5]

Systemic gaps (appearing in >50% of tickets):
  [Gap 1 — specific]
  [Gap 2]

Top improvement priority: [A1/A2/A3/A4/A5] — [what to fix and how]

INDIVIDUAL TICKET SCORES
─────────────────────────
[Table: Ticket ID | Type | A1 | A2 | A3 | A4 | A5 | Overall | Top fix]

  [Improving / Stable / Declining — with evidence if available]

**Tone:** Process-focused, bottleneck-oriented, non-blaming.
**Length:** One page. Board health + per-ticket flow anomalies.

BOARD FLOW REPORT — [Sprint Name] | [Date]
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

FLOW HEALTH

Stage bottleneck: [stage] — avg [X days] vs expected [Y days]
Tickets with regressions: [N] ([X%])
Review health: [HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW] — avg wait [X hours]

IMPLEMENTATION DELTA
─────────────────────
Tickets with CodeRabbit analysis: [N]
AC coverage rate: [X%]
Contradictions found: [N] — [list ticket IDs]
Unspecified scope additions: [N]

BUG BREAKDOWN
──────────────
Functional: [N] | Technical: [N] | Regression: [N] | UX-reclassify: [N]
Bug density: [X bugs/story pt]
Regressions caught in review: [N] | Missed in review: [N]

FLOW ANOMALIES (tickets needing discussion):
  [PROJ-ID]: "[Title]" — [specific anomaly: N regressions / stalled in X /
              AC contradiction / regression bug not caught in review]
  
  [Continue for each anomaly]

TOP 3 PROCESS IMPROVEMENTS:
  1. [Specific, actionable, evidence-grounded]
  2. [Specific]
  3. [Specific]
