# Feedback Report Template

The Feedback Synthesiser agent uses this template at the end of every session.
Fill every section. Do not skip sections — if a dimension was not assessed, say so and explain why.
Write in direct, professional language. This is not a performance review. It is a professional development document.

## Report writing guidelines

**On scores:** Be honest. A score of 3 is not bad — it means the candidate meets expectations.
Do not round up because the candidate tried hard. Do not round down because they were nervous.
Score what you actually observed.

**On the hire signal:** This is the most important field. Do not make it ambiguous.
"Borderline" is a valid answer but must be explained. "Hire with reservations" is not a valid answer.

**On the gaps section:** Name the gap directly. Not "could improve analytical communication"
but "could not do back-of-envelope unit economics under time pressure without a calculator."

**On the roadmap:** Every action must be something a person can actually do next Monday.
Not "develop platform product thinking" but "map one product you use daily as if it were a
platform — identify the config surface, the extension points, and the multi-tenant model."

**On the "one honest thing":** This is the most useful part of the report.
It should say something the candidate is unlikely to hear elsewhere.
It should be kind in intent and direct in delivery.
Do not use it to repeat a gap you already named. Use it to say something true and specific
about how this person's career trajectory is going — and what would change if they fixed
the one thing that matters most.

**Tone throughout:** Professional, direct, respectful.
Not warm. Not cold. The tone of a senior colleague who takes you seriously enough to tell you the truth.
